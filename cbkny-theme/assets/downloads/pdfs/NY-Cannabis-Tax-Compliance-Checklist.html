280E Compliance Checklist

IRC 280E Tax Compliance Requirements

COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) Tracking
Direct Materials Tracking
Seeds, soil, nutrients, packaging materials, and other direct production costs are properly categorized and tracked.
Direct Labor Allocation
Growing, harvesting, processing, and quality control labor costs are properly allocated to COGS.
Production Facility Costs
Facility rent, utilities, and maintenance costs are properly allocated between COGS and non-deductible expenses.
Inventory Valuation Method
Consistent inventory valuation method (FIFO, LIFO, or weighted average) is documented and applied consistently.
Non-Deductible Expense Identification
Marketing & Advertising
All marketing, advertising, and promotional expenses are identified as non-deductible under 280E.
Administrative Expenses
General administrative costs, office expenses, and management fees are properly categorized as non-deductible.
Professional Services
Legal, accounting, and consulting fees are properly categorized based on their purpose (COGS vs non-deductible).

OCM Reporting Checklist

New York Office of Cannabis Management Requirements

Seed-to-Sale Tracking System
METRC Integration
METRC (Marijuana Enforcement Tracking Reporting & Compliance) system is properly integrated and maintained.
Inventory Reconciliation
Monthly inventory reconciliation between METRC and accounting records is performed and documented.
Transaction Reporting
All cannabis transactions are properly recorded in METRC and reconciled with accounting records.
Monthly Reporting Requirements
Sales Reporting
Monthly sales reports are submitted to OCM by the 15th of each month for the previous month.
Inventory Reporting
Monthly inventory reports are submitted to OCM with accurate quantities and valuations.
Financial Reporting
Required financial reports are submitted to OCM on time and with accurate information.
License Compliance
License Renewal Tracking
All cannabis licenses are tracked for renewal dates and requirements.
Compliance Officer Designation
A designated compliance officer is appointed and trained on OCM requirements.
